Physics Behind Hot Air Balloons

Archimedes' Principle: A hot air balloon rises because the hot air inside is less dense than the cooler air outside. This creates a buoyant force that lifts the balloon.

Key Formulas:

Air Density: ρ = P / (R × T)

  • ρ = air density (kg/m³)
  • P = air pressure (Pa)
  • R = specific gas constant for air (287 J/kg·K)
  • T = temperature (K)

Buoyant Force: F_b = ρ_outside × V × g

Net Lift: F_net = F_buoyant - (m_balloon + m_payload) × g

When the net lift is positive, the balloon rises. When negative, it descends. The pilot controls this by heating or cooling the air inside the balloon envelope.
